When was the last time someone held you without you having to earn it?

Not a partner. Not a friend needing something back. Just warmth. Just presence. Just skin.

Most adults haven't been held like that in years. That's not a personal flaw. It's how our culture works. Touch got tangled up with sex, and the simple kind of closeness quietly disappeared along the way.

The Cuddle Nest brings it back.

What it is

A facilitated, non-sexual cuddle gathering. A warm room, soft mats on the floor, gentle light, quiet music, snacks, and a small group of humans being close without agenda. The way kids and animals do it.

A structured, safe space to experience what cuddling feels like when nobody is performing anything, and when every touch comes from an honest yes.

Why it's safe

The flow is responsive, the frame is firm. Clear concepts, tested consent language, and three principles I hold without exception.

01

Consent first

Every touch is asked for out loud. "May I put my hand on your shoulder?" You say yes, or you say no. That's it. No explaining. No softening.

02

A full "no" is welcome

You will practice saying no before you ever touch anyone. That's the real gift. Learning your body can refuse without guilt, and still be welcome in the room.

03

I hold the space

I set the frame, explain the rules, read the room, and adjust the flow so everyone feels held. You can tap me anytime during the evening.
